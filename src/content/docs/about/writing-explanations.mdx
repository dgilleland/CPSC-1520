In many ways, your explanation document is like an article that allows the reader to

- Get a "behind-the-scenes" look at *why* things are the way they are.
- Dive deeper into understanding how things work or what considerations you might want to keep in mind to "up your game".

## [Explanations](https://diataxis.fr/explanation/#the-language-of-explanation)

- `The reason for x is because historically, y …`
  - Explain.
- `W is better than z, because …`
  - Offer judgements and even opinions where appropriate..
- `An x in system y is analogous to a w in system z. However …`
  - Provide context that helps the reader.
- `Some users prefer w (because z). This can be a good approach, but…`
  - Weigh up alternatives.
- `An x interacts with a y as follows: …`
  - Unfold the machinery’s internal secrets, to help understand why something does what it does.
